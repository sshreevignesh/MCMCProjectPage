City Scale Pollution Aware Traffic Routing using MCMC methods

Machine Learning Lab, IIIT Hyderabad, India

Abstract

A significant cause of air pollution in urban areas worldwide is the high volume of road traffic. Long-term exposure to severe pollution can cause serious health issues. One approach towards tackling this problem is to design a pollution-aware traffic routing policy that balances multiple objectives of i) avoiding extreme pollution in any area ii) enabling short transit times, and iii) making effective use of the road capacities. We propose a novel sampling-based approach for this problem. We provide the first construction of a Markov Chain that can sample integer max flow solutions of a planar graph, with theoretical guarantees that the probabilities depend on the aggregate transit length. We designed a traffic policy using diverse samples and simulated traffic on real-world road maps using the SUMO traffic simulator. We observe a considerable decrease in areas with severe pollution when experimented with maps of large cities across the world compared to other approaches.

 

Overview

Air pollution is a major health issue affecting people worldwide. Outdoor air pollution causes millions of deaths each year due to various diseases. Low and middle-income countries are particularly affected, with road traffic being a significant contributor to air pollution in urban areas. To combat this, a transportation policy that distributes traffic flow more evenly through a city is proposed using k-optimality, which ensures that any two flows have at most ≤ k edges in common. A new approach called MaxFlow-MCMC is developed using a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) based method to sample integer max flow solutions from a planar graph and generate a k-Optimal set of max flow solutions. This approach is scalable to large-scale cities and was tested on real-world road networks of multiple cities, showing a significant decrease in normalized mean pollution compared to previous algorithms. The MaxFlow-MCMC method offers a diverse set of paths, preventing the concentration of pollution.
